# Commercial Licensing

wuwei is free software under the [GNU Affero General Public License, version 3 or
later](./LICENSE) (AGPL-3.0-or-later). For most open-source and internal use, the
AGPL is all you need, and it costs nothing.

This page is for the case where the AGPL's terms **don't** fit.

## When you need a commercial license

The AGPL is strong copyleft with a network clause. If you **modify wuwei and run
it to provide a service over a network**, the AGPL requires you to offer the
complete corresponding source of your modified version to that service's users.
Redistributing wuwei as part of a larger work carries the same obligation. A
commercial license may fit if you want to:

- **Embed wuwei in a closed-source product** you distribute, without releasing
  your own source under the AGPL.
- **Run a modified wuwei as a network service** without the AGPL's requirement to
  publish your modifications to users.

If you're using stock wuwei internally, building open-source software, or
contributing back, you almost certainly do **not** need a commercial license.

## What a commercial license grants

A commercial license grants **relief from the AGPL's copyleft and
network-disclosure obligations** for the agreed scope. It does **not** by itself
include a warranty, support, indemnity, or an SLA — those can be negotiated
separately but are not implied. wuwei's technical claim is unchanged by any
license: *the model can propose anything; it can execute only what the gate proves
permitted.* A commercial license changes your **licensing** obligations, not what
the software guarantees.

**Note — wuwei runs on [Rusty](https://github.com/TheLakeMan/rusty)**, which is
itself AGPL-3.0-or-later and dual-licensed by the same author. A commercial
deployment that embeds Rusty can be licensed together — mention your setup in the
inquiry and we'll cover both.
